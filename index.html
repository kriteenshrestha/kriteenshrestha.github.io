About Me

Hello! My name is Kriteen Shrestha, and I am a 22 year old Nepali-American, born and raised in San Jose, California.

I earned my B.S. in Computer Engineering and B.S. in Mathematics (double majored!), both from UC Santa Barbara in 2025, and now am continuing as a Masterâ€™s student in the ECE BS/MS program at UCSB.

I grew up a very curious kid, and I developed interests in a wide range of areas: from math, art, music, physics, baking, and crafts. My mom always let me and my sister get involved in the kitchen no matter how young we were. And so as I grew older, I felt comfortable in the kitchen, and fell in love with cooking and baking, making food and pleasing people with my food.

With art and music running in the family, I grew up really enjoying various art forms and music. I played the trumpet in school, and fell in love with creating and self studying music, and felt determined to learn as many instruments as I could on my own. Little by little, I taught myself guitar, piano, flute, kalimba, and more -- and just throwing notes into melodies became a destresser for me. Arts and crafts was also a favorite of mine. Throughout my teenage years, I raced through as many crafts as you can think of: bracelet making (shoutout RBL!), drawing, painting, crocheting, jewelery resin making, calligraphy, embroidery -- the list goes on. One of these forms of art that stuck with me was handlettering, which became incorporated heavily in my journaling.

Ever since I was 8 years old, I have kept a journal that has evolved into many different types of journals, from doodles to bullet journal planning style. It has become my source of expression and enabled me to brainstorm, rant, and heal myself during hard times through entries of art and brain dumps.

With my passions in art and music, in school I gravitated a lot towards mathematics. My father, being a physics enthusiast and researcher, showed us how fun these subjects could be, so I had a very positive and enjoyable perspective towards these areas.

As I entered college, I found my calling in the intersection of math, physics, and computer engineering, and I love it.
